Charles Bradley

The Screaming Eagle of Soul

    Timeline

  • 1948 - Born on November 5th in Gainesville, Florida
  • 1949 - Abandoned by his mother, who leaves him to be raised by his maternal grandmother ( "She was like a mother to me" )
  • 1956 - Reunited with his mother, who takes him to live with her in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1962 - His sister takes him to see James Brown for the first time at the Apollo Theater, inspiring him to practice singing and dancing like his new idol
  • 1963 - Runs away from home at the age of fourteen to escape poor living conditions and spends the next two years living on the streets and sleeping in subway cars
  • 1965 - Enlists in the Job Corps, moving to Virginia then New Jersey before finally ending up in Bar Harbor, Maine, where he spends the next 10 years working as a chef. It was during this time that he first started to perform, overcoming his natural shyness after being pushed through the stage curtains by a crew-member
  • 1977 - After deciding to hitchhike West across the continent and living in upstate New York, Seattle, Alaska and Canada, he eventually settles in Northern California, working odd jobs and supplementing his income by playing small shows, going by stage names such as The Screaming Eagle of Soul and James Brown Jr.
  • 1996 - 33 years after running away from home, he receives a telephone call from his mother: "Son, give me a chance to know you. Come on back home." He returns to Brooklyn and begins to make a living moonlighting in local clubs as a James Brown impersonator, but struggles with ill health and almost dies of an allergic reaction to penicillin. He recovers with the support of his brother Joseph
  • 1999 - Awakened to the sound of sirens outside their mother's house to discover that Joseph has been tragically murdered by a gunshot to the head just a few streets away
  • 2001 - Encouraged by a to-this-day unidentified friend, he knocks on the door of Daptone Records co-founder Gabriel Roth's apartment in Williamsburg and says "I heard you were looking for a singer"...
  • 2002 - The single "Take It As It Come, Pts 1 & 2" by The Sugarman 3 & Co. featuring Charles Bradley is released on Daptone Records
  • 2003 - He meets Tommy "TNT" Brenneck, forging a friendship and musical partnership that leads to a series of singles and live shows over the next 10 years
  • 2011 - At the age of 62, his debut album No Time For Dreaming is released to critical acclaim, named as one of Rolling Stone's Top Fifty Albums of 2011, and Charles Bradley tours the world as a headline act
  • 2012 - A documentary of his life story, Soul of America directed by Poull Brien, debuts at the SXSW Film Festival in Austin, Texas
  • 2013 - His second album Victim Of Love is released on Daptone Records
  • 2016 - Changes, his third full-length release comes out on April 1st, dedicated to the memory of his mother who had passed in January 2014. Later that year, Bradley is forced to cut short his UK tour due to illness. He is diagnosed with stomach cancer
  • 2017 - On September 23rd, Charles Edward Bradley passes away in Brooklyn, New York at the age of 68, surrounded by family, friends and bandmates

"With all the hurts they've been through, they find a loving way and a way to put their mind on something greater. That's what makes a good artist, a good singer: because you can hear the hurt in their voice, and you can hear the love in their voice."

- Charles Edward Bradley
